# Sprint Plan: SaaS Churn Dashboard

## Purpose
This sprint plan shows how the SaaS churn and revenue-at-risk dashboard can be delivered using Scrum. The work is divided into four sprints, moving from requirements and data understanding to dashboard delivery, testing and final recommendations.

## Sprint Overview

| Sprint | Focus Area | Main Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Sprint 1 | Requirements and planning | Stakeholder needs, business questions and backlog are defined. |
| Sprint 2 | Data preparation and churn analysis | Clean data and churn metrics are prepared. |
| Sprint 3 | Revenue-at-risk analysis and dashboard build | Revenue-risk calculations and Power BI visuals are created. |
| Sprint 4 | Testing, review and final delivery | Dashboard is validated and final recommendations are documented. |

## Sprint 1: Requirements and Planning

### Sprint Goal
Define stakeholder needs, dashboard scope, key metrics and project backlog.

### Sprint Tasks
- Identify target stakeholders.
- Document stakeholder requirements.
- Define functional and non-functional requirements.
- Define churn rate, monthly revenue at risk and annual revenue at risk.
- Create epics and product backlog.
- Confirm success criteria.

### Deliverables
- Project charter
- Stakeholder requirements document
- Epics and product backlog
- Initial user stories and acceptance criteria

### Sprint Review Notes
Sprint 1 is successful if the project scope is clear, stakeholder needs are documented and backlog items are ready for delivery planning.

## Sprint 2: Data Preparation and Churn Analysis

### Sprint Goal
Prepare the SaaS datasets and calculate churn-related metrics.

### Sprint Tasks
- Review customer, subscription, usage, support and churn datasets.
- Check missing values, duplicates and data types.
- Prepare SQL or Python analysis workflow.
- Calculate overall customer churn rate.
- Analyse churn by industry, plan tier and acquisition source.
- Identify high-risk customer segments.

### Deliverables
- Cleaned data workflow
- Churn rate calculation
- Customer segment churn analysis
- Initial churn insight summary

### Sprint Review Notes
Sprint 2 is successful if churn metrics are calculated, validated and ready to support dashboard visuals.

## Sprint 3: Revenue-at-Risk Analysis and Dashboard Build

### Sprint Goal
Calculate revenue exposure from churn and build the main Power BI dashboard.

### Sprint Tasks
- Calculate monthly revenue at risk.
- Calculate annual revenue at risk.
- Analyse revenue exposure by plan tier.
- Design dashboard layout and KPI structure.
- Build churn visuals in Power BI.
- Build revenue-at-risk visuals in Power BI.
- Add segment filters for stakeholder exploration.

### Deliverables
- Revenue-at-risk calculations
- Dashboard wireframe or layout plan
- Power BI dashboard draft
- Churn and revenue-risk visuals

### Sprint Review Notes
Sprint 3 is successful if the dashboard draft communicates churn performance, revenue exposure and key risk segments clearly.

## Sprint 4: Testing, Review and Final Delivery

### Sprint Goal
Validate dashboard outputs, improve usability and document final recommendations.

### Sprint Tasks
- Validate KPI calculations against SQL or Python outputs.
- Test dashboard filters and visuals.
- Review dashboard clarity from a stakeholder perspective.
- Document assumptions and limitations.
- Write final business recommendations.
- Create final delivery summary.
- Complete sprint review and retrospective notes.

### Deliverables
- Validated Power BI dashboard
- Final recommendation summary
- Sprint review notes
- Retrospective summary
- Final delivery summary

### Sprint Review Notes
Sprint 4 is successful if dashboard outputs are accurate, easy to interpret and linked to clear retention recommendations.

## Sprint Timeline

| Sprint | Estimated Duration | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Sprint 1 | 1 week | Requirements and planning |
| Sprint 2 | 1 week | Data preparation and churn analysis |
| Sprint 3 | 1 week | Revenue-at-risk analysis and dashboard build |
| Sprint 4 | 1 week | Testing, review and final delivery |

## Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Unclear stakeholder requirements | Dashboard may miss business needs | Define requirements before building visuals. |
| Incorrect metric definitions | Dashboard numbers may be misleading | Validate metrics against source calculations. |
| Dashboard becomes too technical | Stakeholders may not use it effectively | Use clear labels, KPI cards and business-language insights. |
| Scope creep | Delivery may become unfocused | Prioritise core churn and revenue-risk features first. |
| Weak recommendations | Analysis may not support decisions | Link every recommendation to a specific insight. |

## Summary
The sprint plan shows how the analytics dashboard can be delivered in a structured Agile process. Each sprint has a clear goal, planned tasks, deliverables and review criteria.
