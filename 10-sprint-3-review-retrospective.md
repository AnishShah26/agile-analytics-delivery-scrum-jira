## Sprint Review

During Sprint 3, the financial impact of churn was analysed through monthly and annual revenue-at-risk calculations. This helped translate customer churn into business impact for revenue and leadership stakeholders.

The sprint also focused on building the executive dashboard view, including KPI cards, churn visuals, revenue-risk visuals and filters. The dashboard was designed to support clear stakeholder decision-making rather than technical reporting only.

## Sprint Outcome

Sprint 3 was completed successfully. All 3 planned stories were moved to Done in Jira, completing 8 out of 8 planned story points.

## What Went Well

- Churn analysis was connected to revenue impact.
- The dashboard structure focused on executive-level decision-making.
- Filters improved stakeholder exploration of churn and revenue risk.
- The sprint created a strong visual reporting layer for the project.

## What Could Be Improved

- Dashboard wireframes could be created earlier before building visuals.
- Revenue-at-risk assumptions should be reviewed with stakeholders before final use.
- More user testing could improve dashboard clarity and usability.

## Lessons Learned

This sprint showed that analytics becomes more valuable when technical findings are translated into financial and business impact. Revenue-at-risk analysis helps stakeholders understand why churn matters and where action should be prioritised.
